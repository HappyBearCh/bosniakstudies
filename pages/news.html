Bosniak Studies — From an Idea to a Global Movement: "We Are Not Aware of Our Rich History"

Hayat.ba — Bosniak Studies in the Media

Bosniak Studies is a leading academic platform dedicated to exploring Bosniak communities worldwide. Researchers and scholars work together to advance knowledge of Bosniak history, culture, and society through a network spanning Bosnia and Herzegovina, Sandžak, Kosovo, North Macedonia, Turkey, and the diaspora.

The goal is to preserve heritage and strengthen understanding within the communities where Bosniaks live. Anyone interested can get involved through the official website bosniakstudies.org — as a member or as an author of research. The platform and all content are available in English, with the aim of connecting experts and audiences from around the world.

Esad Širbegović, Director of Public Relations, described how the platform has brought together people from five different countries — academics whose work focuses on Bosniaks. Through Bosniak Studies, he says, he has personally learned many things he had not been aware of before.

"This is the Bosniak Studies platform — Bosniak Studies have not existed anywhere until now, neither in Bosnia nor in the world. We built it as a grassroots movement, from the bottom up. We connected people from various countries — the United States, the United Kingdom, Poland, Greece — making it a large platform where academics who study Bosniaks come together. We send letters, emails, publish articles, publish academic papers, and publish pieces in the media. Through Bosniak Studies I have personally learned many things I was not aware of — about Bosniaks, about our rich history which, unfortunately, under the old system, we never had the chance to learn about. Through Bosniak Studies, all of that becomes possible."

— Esad Širbegović, Director of Public Relations, Bosniak Studies

Call for Papers: Handbook of Bosniak Studies

Deadline: Friday, May 1, 2026

The Bosniak Studies Association invites chapter proposals for the first ever Handbook of Bosniak Studies, a comprehensive academic volume under the theme "Bosniaks: Past, Present, and Future of a Southeastern European Nation."

This landmark publication aims to bring together scholars and researchers from a wide range of disciplines to introduce the Bosniak nation across time and space — from ancient history to contemporary diaspora communities worldwide.

Key Information:

  • Abstract deadline: May 1, 2026
  • Abstracts: 300–500 words, submitted as a Word document
  • Include: CV and institutional affiliation of all contributors
  • Submit to: Mkrijest@fiu.edu
  • Editors: Mirsad Kriještorac, Ph.D. & Sabina Pačariz, Ph.D.
