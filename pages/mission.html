Our Mission

Mission Statement

Bosniak Studies aims to become a leader in the academic exploration of all issues relevant to Bosniaks in Bosnia and Herzegovina, Sandžak, Kosovo, Macedonia, Türkiye, and the global diaspora.

Our mission is to foster scholarly interest, generate and disseminate knowledge about Bosniak individuals and communities, Bosniak culture, traditions, scientific contributions, institutions, religion, history, geography, language, economics, sports, healthcare, and other disciplines that reflect the Bosniak experience.

Our Guiding Philosophy

"There is no guide like reason, no guardian like justice, no sword like truth, and no aid like sincerity."

— Hasan Kafi al-Aqhisari (Hasan Kafi Pruščak)

This profound wisdom from the great Bosniak philosopher guides all of our work. We are committed to pursuing truth through rigorous analysis, ensuring justice in our representation of Bosniak history and culture, and maintaining sincerity in all our scholarly endeavors.

Our Objectives

01

Advance Knowledge

Generate cutting-edge scholarly research that advances understanding of Bosniak communities and their contributions to global civilization.

02

Build Community

Create platforms for scholars, researchers, and community members to collaborate, exchange ideas, and engage in meaningful dialogue.

03

Preserve Heritage

Document, preserve, and celebrate Bosniak cultural heritage for current and future generations.

04

Promote Understanding

Foster cross-cultural understanding and promote awareness of Bosniak history, culture, and contributions globally.

05

Support Research

Provide resources, funding, and support for high-quality research on all aspects of Bosniak life and society.

06

Foster Excellence

Maintain the highest standards of academic rigor and scholarly excellence in all our endeavors.

Scope of Work

Bosniak Studies encompasses a wide range of disciplines and areas of focus, including:

  • History and Historical Analysis
  • Culture and Cultural Studies
  • Language and Linguistics
  • Religion and Theology
  • Philosophy and Ethics
  • Literature and Literary Arts
  • Geography and Geopolitics
  • Economics and Development
  • Social Sciences
  • Science and Technology
  • Arts and Architecture
  • Contemporary Issues
